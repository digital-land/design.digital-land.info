Introduction

For the RIPA service to determine if planning permission is needed for a development, it needs to know whether there are any planning constraints that relate to the site of the planning application.

RIPA will get this information from the Digital Land platform which in turn will be collecting data from local planning authorities and national sources such as Historic England, ensuring that it has the most up-to-date information.

Datasets you need to publish

  • Article 4 directions
  • Conservation areas
  • Listed buildings and locally listed buildings (shape files)
  • Tree preservation orders

How to publish your data

Your data needs to be on a url or website that is open to the public. The Digital Land platform will then access that data and routinely collect anything that is new or has changed. The platform can consume data in different formats, you just need to ensure its published.

Data fields you can provide

For each dataset there are certain required fields as well as some additional information that will make the dataset more complete.
